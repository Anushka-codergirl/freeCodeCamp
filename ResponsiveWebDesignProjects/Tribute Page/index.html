Who Is Bill Gates?

Entrepreneur and businessman Bill Gates (born October 28, 1955) and his partner Paul Allen founded and built the world's largest software business, Microsoft, through technological innovation, keen business strategy and aggressive business tactics. In the process, Gates became one of the richest men in the world. In February 2014, Gates announced that he was stepping down as Microsoft's chairman to focus on charitable work at his foundation, the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation.

    Bill's Career:

  • 1967 - Enrolled at Lakeside School. First used computer.
  • 1968 - Began programming with Paul Allen in the computer center.
  • 1973 - Entered Harvard University.
  • 1975 - Started Micro-Soft with Paul Allen in Albuquerque, NM.
  • 1976 - Changed company name to Microsoft.
  • 1979 - Microsoft moved to Washington State.
  • 1985 - Windows 1.0 launched.
  • 1995 - Windows 95 launched.
  • 2000 - Assumed role of Chief Software Architect.
  • 2001 - The original Xbox released.
  • 2008 - Left his daily job at Microsoft.
  • 2014 - Stepped down as chairman. Began serving as technology advisor.

Philanthrophy:

  • 2000 - Bill and Melinda officially established the foundation.
  • 2002 - The foundation completed efforts to help install 47,000 computers in 11,000 libraries in all 50 states.
  • 2006 - The foundation completed efforts to help install 47,000 computers in 11,000 libraries in all 50 states.
  • 2010 - Bill and Melinda challenged the global health community to declare this the Decade of Vaccines. They pledged $10 billion over the next 10 years to vaccines.
  • 2013- Bill helped launch a $5.5 billion effort to eradicate polio by 2018.
